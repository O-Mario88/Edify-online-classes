# Edify — A Complete Digital School Platform for Africa

---

## The Problem

Across East Africa, schools still run on paper registers, handwritten report cards, and disconnected WhatsApp groups. Teachers lack tools to track which students are falling behind. Parents receive updates once a term — if at all. School finances live in spreadsheets that don't talk to each other.

Meanwhile, millions of students preparing for national exams like Uganda's UNEB, Kenya's KCSE, and Rwanda's national examinations have no structured way to track their readiness or access quality study materials outside the classroom.

**The result:** Students slip through the cracks. Schools lose revenue to poor fee tracking. Teachers burn out without support. Parents stay in the dark.

---

## What Is Edify?

Edify is an all-in-one digital school platform built specifically for East African schools. It gives every person in the school community — students, teachers, parents, and administrators — a personalised dashboard and the tools they need, all in one place.

Think of it as **a school operating system**: learning, teaching, administration, finance, and communication — unified under one roof.

---

## Key Features at a Glance

### 1. Smart Student Dashboards
Every student sees their progress across all subjects in real time. The platform highlights weak areas, suggests resources, and shows a clear readiness score for upcoming national exams (PLE, UCE, KCSE, and others). Students earn points and badges as they learn — making education feel rewarding.

### 2. Teacher Intelligence Tools
Teachers get alerts when a student is at risk of falling behind. An AI assistant helps draft lesson plans, generate quizzes, and write parent communication letters. Teachers can upload resources, manage attendance, and track which teaching materials are actually working.

### 3. Parent Visibility
Parents no longer wait until the end of term to know how their child is doing. They see real-time attendance, subject-by-subject performance, and can communicate directly with teachers — all from their phone.

### 4. School Finance & ERP
A fully integrated finance module built for how African schools actually operate:
- **Fee management** — Set up tuition, boarding, transport, exam, and other fee categories
- **Student billing & collections** — Track who has paid, who owes, and send automated reminders
- **Bursaries & discounts** — Configure merit-based and needs-based scholarships
- **Expense tracking** — Record school expenses with a digital cashbook
- **Financial reports** — Generate income statements and balance sheets at the click of a button
- **Mobile money integration** — Accept payments via MTN Mobile Money, Airtel Money, and bank transfers

### 5. Timetable & Academic Management
An intelligent timetable builder that detects scheduling conflicts automatically. Schools can manage classes, terms, subjects, and teacher assignments — no more wall charts and sticky notes.

### 6. National Exam Readiness
Purpose-built exam trackers for:
- **Uganda:** PLE (Primary), UCE & UACE (Secondary) — aligned to UNEB standards
- **Kenya:** KCSE — aligned to KNEC standards
- **Rwanda:** O-Level & A-Level — aligned to REB standards

Students, teachers, and parents can all see exactly how prepared a learner is for their national exams — weeks or months in advance.

### 7. Content Marketplace
Independent teachers can create and sell courses on the platform. Schools can curate private resource libraries. Students can access a growing catalogue of videos, documents, and interactive content organised by country, subject, and topic.

### 8. AI Teaching Assistant
An AI copilot that lives inside the platform and helps teachers:
- Auto-generate quizzes from any topic
- Summarise class performance in plain language
- Suggest which students need immediate attention
- Draft parent letters and reports

### 9. Primary & Secondary Coverage
Edify covers the full school journey:
- **Primary school** (P4–P7) with PLE readiness tracking, age-appropriate dashboards, and parent home-support guides
- **Secondary school** (S1–S6 / Form 1–4) with full subject management, exam readiness, and career guidance

### 10. Built for Offline Use
Designed with African connectivity in mind. The platform works even when the internet drops — data syncs automatically when the connection returns.

## How It Works for a School

**Week 1 — Setup**
A 10-step onboarding wizard walks the school through setup: create the school profile, add branding, configure classes and subjects, invite teachers, enrol students, and connect parent accounts.

**Day-to-Day**
- Teachers mark attendance, deliver lessons, upload marks, and get AI-powered insights
- Students log in to study, take practice tests, and track their exam readiness
- Parents check in on their child's progress from any device
- The admin team manages finances, timetables, and school operations from one dashboard

**Term End**
Financial reports, academic reports, and exam readiness summaries are generated automatically — ready to print or share digitally.

## Revenue Model

Edify operates on a sustainable hybrid model:

1. **School Subscriptions** — Schools pay a termly or annual fee for the full platform
2. **Marketplace Commission** — The platform earns a percentage on course sales by independent teachers
3. **Transaction Fees** — Small fees on financial transactions processed through the platform
4. **Premium Features** — Advanced analytics, AI tools, and priority support for larger institutions

## The Opportunity

- **Over 100,000 schools** across Uganda, Kenya, and Rwanda
- **Most still run on paper** — digital adoption is early but accelerating
- **Government push** for digital learning (post-COVID momentum, CBC in Kenya, ICT in education policies)
- **Mobile money penetration** above 50% across the region — parents can pay fees from their phones
- **Young, tech-ready population** — median age under 20 in all three countries

Edify is not competing with Western EdTech platforms that don't understand African school operations. It is built from the ground up for how schools in this region actually work — including the financial realities, exam systems, curriculum structures, and connectivity challenges.
